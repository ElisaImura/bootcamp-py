AMIGURUMI (編み包み)

An acronym in Japanese, composed of: ami, "knitting", and nuigurumi, "stuffed doll", is a Japanese trend or fashion that consists of weaving small dolls using crochet or crochet techniques. The amigurumis are mainly in the form of animals such as bears, rabbits, cats or dogs, but they also take on other forms such as food, plants and even accessories such as bags, jewelry, etc. In Japan, amigurumi is more than just a simple pastime, since it is part of the kawaii culture, a term that could be translated as "cute" or "cute". It allows you to create characters and imitate objects from real life, but with a touch cuddly and adorable. These types of activities stimulate creativity and improve skills to produce more and better things. Thanks to the ease of crochet, it makes this one of the most chosen techniques when starting a craft venture.
